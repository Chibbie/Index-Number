Index Numbers

Index Numbers
Intro
Index numbers
convenient devices for measuring relative changes of differences from time to time or place to place
used to represent a set of values over two or more different time periods or localities
ratio of two or more time periods one of which is base time period and the its value serves as the standard point of comparison
Index number time series, series of index numbers for two or more time periods where the base year is same
Relatives
because absolute numbers are of little importance and meaningless in themselves.
less unwieldy and readily understandable
Types
Simple
Single variable
Composite
Two or more variables.
Commonly this is the case
Issues Involved
Selection of data
Random sampling or a stratified random sampling is to be done to ensure comparability of data
Base Period
carefully selected because it is a point of reference in comparing various data describing individual behavior. Period should be normal i.e. one of relative stability. Should be relatively recent. three variants = Fixed, chain and average
Selection of Weights
to point out each variable involved in composite index shoud have a reasonable influence on the index.
Use of averages
Geometric mean is better in averaging relatives but for most of the indices AM is used for its simplicity
Choice of variables
Such as wholesale prices / retail prices etc.
Selection of formula
Since different types of indices give different values when applied to the same data.
Methods
Simple Aggregative Method
If we don't use relatives and use absolute quantities then such price quotations become the concealed weights which have no logical significance
Simple Average of Relatives
Flaw: gives equal importance to each of the relatives. amounts to giving undue weight to a commodity which is used in a small quantity because the relatives which have no regard to the absolute quantity will give more weight than what is due from the quantity used.
Weighted Method
Laspeyres' Index : Base year Q as weights
Paasche's Index : Current year Q as weights
Methods based on some typical period
Marshall Edgeworth Index: taking average of the base year and the current year
Fisher's Ideal price index: Geometric Mean of Laspeyres' and Paasche's.
Weighted Average of Relative Method: Generally weighted AM is used although weighted geometric mean can also be used
Usefulness of Index Numbers
Limitations
constructed from samples so errors can creep in
based on selected items so depict only broad trend not real picture
many methods = many results = confusion
Usefulness
guidelines to make decisions in measuring IQ, research etc.
reveal trends and tendencies in making important conclusions in cyclical forces, irregular forces, etc.
important in forecasting future economic activity. To study long-term trend
very useful in deflating. Transform nominal wages to real wages
Cost of living index measure changes in the cost of living over a given period
Test of Adequacy
Unit Test
requires that formula be independent of units. All tests pass this except Simple Aggregative Index
Time Reversal Test:
determine whether a given method will work both ways in time
Two ratioes the current on the base and the base on the current should multiply into unity
Only Fisher's index satisfy this test
Factor Reversal Test
Product of Price and Q index should be equal to Value index
Only Fisher
Circular Test
Test of shiftability of base
Only the GM of price relatives and weighted aggregative with fixed weights meet this test.
